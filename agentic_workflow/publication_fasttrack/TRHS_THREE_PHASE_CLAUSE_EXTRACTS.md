## Phase 1 — Measurement (Geometry/Numeric)

## Cl. 2.1 HS OR NS FORM (Page 3)
> The configuration of a HS or NS on plan shall be rectangular, square, trapezoidal or L-shaped.

## Cl. 2.2.1 Area and Volume (Page 3)
> (a) The maximum internal length of any floor and roof slab of a HS shall be 4000mm. The minimum internal width of a HS shall be 1200mm. The internal length and width of HS walls shall be designed with an increment of 50mm. See FIGURE 2.2.1(a).
>
> (b) If the configuration of HS on plan is rectangular or square, the minimum internal floor area and minimum internal volume of a HS shall be in accordance with TABLE 2.2.1(b).
>
> (c) If the configuration of HS on plan is trapezoidal or L-shaped, the minimum internal floor area, minimum internal volume of a HS and the number of 0.6 m x 0.6 m square units shall be as specified in TABLE 2.2.1(c) and as illustrated in FIGURE 2.2.1(a).
>
> (d) The maximum internal floor area of a HS shall be 4.8m2. Internal floor area exceeding 4.8m2 may be allowed subject to the approval from the relevant authority.

## Cl. 2.2.2 Height (Page 3)
> (a) The minimum and maximum HS clear height shall be 2400 mm and 3900 mm respectively. See FIGURE 2.2.2(a).
>
> (b) The maximum NS clear height shall be 3900 mm. Where NS clear height is more than 3900 mm, intermediate RC slab or RC beams shall be provided. The RC beams shall be designed with at least equivalent stiffness to RC slab. If the NS is designed with 2 walls, RC beams shall be provided at the peripheral of NS. See FIGURE 2.2.2(b).

## Cl. 2.3.1 Wall Thickness of HS and NS (Pages 3–4)
> (a) The minimum HS and NS wall thickness shall be in accordance with TABLE 2.3.1(a) for landed development and TABLE 2.3.1(b) for non-landed development.
>
> (b) Wall thickness of any HS or NS within the HS tower shall not be less than the wall thickness of the HS or NS above it.
>
> (c) The minimum thickness of the internal common wall between two adjacent HS for non-landed and landed cluster housing developments shall be 200mm thick. See FIGURE 2.3.1(c).
>
> (d) For landed developments where the two HS are abutting each other, the common wall shall be cast as two separate adjoining walls. See FIGURE 2.3.1(d).
>
> (e) The minimum thickness of the basement HS wall facing a reinforced concrete basement storey wall without any opening within the influence zone shall be 200mm. See FIGURE 2.4.6(a) and (b).
>
> (f) The minimum thickness of the basement HS wall which is in direct contact with earth throughout its entire height shall be in accordance with FIGURE 2.4.6(c).
>
> (g) The minimum thickness of the basement HS wall facing a reinforced concrete basement storey wall with opening within the influence zone shall be in accordance with TABLE 2.3.1(a).

## Cl. 2.3.2 Slab Thickness of HS and Enclosed NS (Page 4)
> (a) Ceiling slab of top-most HS in non-landed development - 300mm. See FIGURE 2.3.2.
>
> (b) Slab between 2 HS - 175mm. See FIGURE 2.3.2.
>
> (c) Floor slab of bottom-most HS without NS below – 200mm. See FIGURE 2.3.2.
>
> (d) Floor slab of bottom-most NS in contact with soil – 200mm.
>
> (e) Slab between 2 enclosed NS – 175mm. See FIGURE 2.3.2.
>
> (f) Slab between HS and enclosed NS – 175mm. See FIGURE 2.3.2.
>
> (g) Ceiling slab of top-most HS in landed development – 300mm or 250mm (if there is a RC roof or another RC slab above the HS that is equal or extend beyond the required setback distance of that HS wall). See FIGURE 2.3.2(f).

## Phase 2 — Spatial & Setback

## Cl. 2.4.1 HS Position (Page 5)
> (a) A HS has to be positioned such that the setback distance of each HS wall shall be as large as practical and shall not be less than the minimum specified setback distance.
>
> (b) A HS with minimum 300mm thick ceiling slab in a landed house may have one of its walls (without HS door) abutting or near to an air well... The area of the air-well shall not be larger than 4.2m2 and the longer side of the air-well shall not be larger than 2.8m. See FIGURE 2.4.1(b).
>
> (c) Where a staircase in a landed house is located within setback distance of the HS wall (without HS door), such staircase can be built of either reinforced concrete or metal/steel or timber materials provided that it is covered with either reinforced concrete roof or clay tile roof. See FIGURE 2.4.1c(i) & c(ii).
>
> (d) Where non-reinforced concrete roof or non-clay tile roof is provided for landed house, there shall be reinforced concrete elements to meet setback distance at least one storey above the HS as stipulated in clauses 2.4.3 and 2.4.4. See FIGURE 2.4.1(d).
>
> (e) Where a non-reinforced concrete lift core in a landed house is located within setback distance of the HS wall (without HS door), such lift core shall be covered with either reinforced concrete roof or clay tile roof and meet the setback distance. See FIGURE 2.4.1e(i) & e(ii).
>
> (f) A HS can also be located in the basement of a landed house... an underground access route leading to HS must be provided with a reinforced concrete ceiling slab of minimum thickness of 125mm.

## Cl. 2.4.6 Setback Distances of Basement HS (Page 8)
> (a) For the HS in the basement, the minimum setback distances of the HS wall with HS door and the remaining 3 HS walls shall comply with TABLE 2.4.6(a).

## Cl. 2.4.8 Void within Setback Distance of HS Walls (Without HS Door) (Page 9)

>

>
> (ii) Clear height from FFL to soffit of ceiling/down-hang beam shall be maximum 2800mm.
>
> (iii) Distance from EBL to edge of staircase landing shall be minimally 1300mm.

## Cl. 2.10 HS Beneath an Internal Staircase (Page 14)
> (a) For minimum internal floor area determination ... only portion with clear height at least 1500mm counted.
>
> (b) For minimum internal volume determination ... entire enclosed space may be used.
>
> (c) Minimum thickness of HS ceiling slab and staircase waist shall be 300mm.
>
> (d) Minimum unobstructed distance from HS wall with ventilation sleeve opening to nearest internal structural element shall be at least 700mm.

## Phase 3 — Components & M&E

## Cl. 2.5 HS Door and Cl. 2.5.2 Door Frame (Pages 10)
> 2.5.1(a) The opening dimensions of HS door shall be 700mm (W) x 1900mm (H).
>
> 2.5.1(b) HS door frame ... shall have single or double door rebate.
>
> 2.5.2(a) There shall be a minimum 150mm reinforced concrete nib next to vertical edge of the HS door frame.
>
> 2.5.2(b)–(d) Requirements for pre-cast door frame panel Types 1, 2, 3.
>
> 2.5.2(e) The door frame must be positioned such that its door is above FFL and can be opened at least 90°.

## Cl. 2.7.1–2.7.4 Fixtures in HS (Page 12)
> 2.7.1(a) Electrical/communication fixtures shall be provided: 13A switched socket outlets; switch and lighting points; communication line for telephony outlet.
>
> 2.7.1(b) Fixtures shall comply with relevant local COPIF and other statutory requirements.
>
> 2.7.1(c) Mounting height shall comply with statutory codes and peacetime requirements.
>
> 2.7.1(d) Other fixtures for peacetime use are allowed if easily dismantled and removed.
>
> 2.7.2 One 13A switched socket outlet in vicinity of telephony outlet + one additional outlet for other appliances.
>
> 2.7.3 Light fittings shall be mounted on soffit of HS ceiling slab using screws with non-metallic inserts; wall-mounted fittings not permitted.
>
> 2.7.4 Cable entry openings shall be fully and properly sealed to ensure air-tightness (as required under Clause 3.6).

## Cl. 3.6.1(e) Electrical Services — Conduit sealing (Page 89)
> A maximum of 4 numbers of 25mm diameter conduits are allowed to be embedded within the HS walls for electrical cables serving fixtures in the HS. Both ends of these conduits on the internal and the external of the HS shall be fully sealed with sealing material in accordance with the manufacturer’s specification and up to a depth of not less than 100mm into the conduits to ensure air-tightness of HS.

## Cl. 4.2 Position (especially 4.2(b)) (Page 164)
> The position of each ventilation sleeve shall comply with:
>
> (a) Height from centre of opening to internal FFL: between 1900mm and 3600mm.
>
> (b) Ventilation sleeve shall have sufficient clearance from structural elements/services and not be located in areas such as toilets, bathroom. Centre to soffit and nearest face of internal HS walls at least 300mm. Minimum unobstructed distance of at least 700mm from ventilation sleeve opening face to nearest internal structural element within HS.
>
> (c) If above/adjacent HS door frame, centre at least 250mm from nearest edge of door frame.
>
> (d) Shortest distance between centres of two ventilation sleeves at least 1000mm.

## Cl. 4.3 Accessibility of Ventilation Sleeves (Page 164)
> 4.3.1 Clearance in front of and around fragmentation plate:
> minimum clearance to RC beam/structure/service shall be 50mm; where fronting fragmentation plate, clear distance at least 500mm.
>
> 4.3.2 False ceiling below ventilation sleeves:
> where false ceilings are provided outside HS and below ventilation sleeves, perforated access panels of minimum 600mm x 600mm shall be positioned directly below each ventilation sleeve.

## Cl. 2.13 Door Recess on HS Wall (Page 15)
> A door recess on HS wall to accommodate protrusion of HS door handle when fully open is allowed provided that:
>
> (a) Dimensions not larger than 160mm (length) x 80mm (height) x 40mm (depth) for HS wall of minimum 250mm thickness.
>
> (b) Spacing between HS door handle recess and external/internal socket points at least 300mm apart.
